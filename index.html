1. India Became a Republic in 1950

On 26 January 1950, India officially became a Republic and adopted its Constitution.

2. The Constitution Replaced British Law

The Government of India Act 1935 was replaced by the Indian Constitution.

3. Dr. B. R. Ambedkar Drafted the Constitution

He served as Chairman of the Drafting Committee and shaped India's legal foundation.

4. Rajendra Prasad Became First President

He took oath as India's first constitutional head of state.

5. 26 January Was Symbolic

The date honored the 1930 Purna Swaraj declaration of independence.

6. Republic Day Parade Began in 1950

The annual parade showcases India's culture, defense, and unity.

7. Gallantry Awards Are Announced

Bravery awards honor citizens and soldiers for extraordinary courage.

8. Beating Retreat Marks the End

Military bands perform a ceremonial closing on 29 January.

9. Netaji Represents Revolutionary Freedom

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose symbolizes resistance and uncompromising patriotism.

10. Republic Means Rule by the People

Power belongs to citizens, guided by law and democratic values.
